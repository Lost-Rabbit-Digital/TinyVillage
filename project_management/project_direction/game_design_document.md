# Tiny Village - Game Design Document

## Executive Summary

**Tiny Village** is a free-to-play cozy village-building and exploration game that combines the base-building satisfaction of Necesse with the simplicity and charm of Beta Minecraft. Players guide the Khoswa-Kian, a small green humanoid species, as they establish and grow their civilization in a procedurally-generated world.

**Target Audience:** Fans of cozy games, casual management sims, and relaxing creativity-focused experiences (think Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing, Minecraft peaceful mode)

**Platforms:** Steam (PC), with potential expansion to console

**Business Model:** Free-to-play base game with ethical DLC content packs and cosmetic microtransactions

---

## Core Pillars

### 1. Cozy & Accessible
- **Low-stress gameplay** with no fail states or time pressure
- **Intuitive controls** suitable for casual players
- **Charming aesthetics** with doodle-themed or hand-drawn art style
- **Relaxing soundtrack** that enhances the zen experience

### 2. Creative Expression
- **Flexible building system** allowing players to design unique villages
- **Decorative freedom** with hundreds of cosmetic options
- **Procedural generation** ensures each playthrough feels fresh
- **Photography/screenshot mode** to share creations

### 3. Meaningful Progression
- **Character advancement** through exploration and discovery
- **Unlockable content** that expands creative possibilities
- **Achievement system** that rewards experimentation
- **No grinding** - progression should feel natural and rewarding

### 4. Simple Depth
- **Easy to learn, engaging to master** core loop
- **Streamlined systems** compared to complex colony sims
- **Optional complexity** for players who want it
- **Clear feedback** on all player actions

## Core Gameplay Loop

```
Explore → Gather → Build → Expand → Customize → Share
     ↑                                              ↓
     ←←←←←←←←←←← Unlock & Discover ←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←←
```

### Core Loop Breakdown

1. **Explore** the procedurally-generated world
   - Discover new biomes, resources, and secrets
   - Find blueprint fragments and special items
   - Encounter friendly NPCs and story events

2. **Gather** resources with your Khoswa-Kian villagers
   - Assign villagers to collection tasks
   - Automatic resource gathering (no tedious clicking)
   - Visual feedback on resource availability

3. **Build** structures and infrastructure
   - Place buildings on a grid-based system
   - Roads, houses, workshops, decorations
   - Each building serves functional or aesthetic purpose

4. **Expand** your village territory
   - Unlock new building types
   - Grow your population
   - Extend into new biomes

5. **Customize** your village aesthetic
   - Color palettes and themes
   - Decorative elements
   - Villager appearance customization

6. **Share** your creations
   - Screenshot mode with filters
   - Export village layouts
   - Community showcases

## Game Systems

### Building & Placement
- **Grid-based placement** for satisfying alignment
- **Snap-to-grid** with optional free-placement mode
- **Rotation support** for all buildings (90° increments)
- **Copy/paste** for repeating patterns
- **Undo/redo** for experimentation without penalty
- **Blueprint system** for saving and loading designs

### Resource Management
- **Simplified resource types:**
  - Basic: Wood, Stone, Metal, Fiber
  - Food: Berries, Crops, Fish
  - Advanced: Alloys, Textiles, Gems
  - Special: Ancient Artifacts, Crystals
- **Automatic collection** - no manual clicking
- **Storage buildings** expand capacity
- **Visual indicators** show resource abundance
- **No resource decay** - stress-free management

### Villager System
- **Population growth** through housing
- **Simple job assignment** (gatherer, builder, researcher)
- **Personality traits** add character variety
- **Happiness system** (visual only, no penalties)
- **Customizable appearance** (clothing, colors, accessories)
- **Simple pathfinding** - villagers navigate automatically

### Exploration & Discovery
- **Procedural world generation** with multiple biomes:
  - Starter: Meadows (lush, green, tutorial area)
  - Forest (wood-rich, mysterious)
  - Desert (hot, ancient ruins)
  - Mountains (mining, elevation mechanics)
  - Ocean/Islands (fishing, boats)
  - Tundra (rare resources, harsh beauty)
  - Mystical (late-game, otherworldly)
- **Points of Interest:**
  - Ancient ruins (lore fragments)
  - Resource deposits
  - NPC settlements
  - Mystery structures
- **No combat requirement** - exploration is peaceful
- **Optional challenges** for players who want them

### Progression System
- **Character levels** unlock new abilities:
  - Building categories
  - Resource types
  - Crafting recipes
  - Villager cap increases
- **Achievement-based unlocks** encourage experimentation
- **Story milestones** gate major content
- **No dead ends** - all paths are viable

### Day/Night & Weather
- **Aesthetic day/night cycle** (no gameplay impact)
- **Dynamic weather** creates atmosphere:
  - Rain (cozy ambiance)
  - Snow (seasonal decoration)
  - Fog (mysterious atmosphere)
  - Clear skies (optimal building)
- **Seasonal events** (optional DLC integration)

## Narrative & Lore

### Story Framework
The game incorporates the **Khoswa-Kian lore** as optional narrative content:

- **Tutorial framing:** Players are the crew of "Tiny Village" ship
- **Environmental storytelling:** Ancient ruins hint at time-loop paradox
- **Collectible lore fragments:** Discover the full story through exploration
- **No forced narrative:** Story is completely optional
- **Multiple endings:** Different story conclusions based on player choices

### Core Story (Optional)
Players can discover that they're part of a time-loop where the Khoswa-Kian sent themselves back to bootstrap their own civilization. This meta-narrative provides:
- Context for building a civilization from scratch
- Explanation for ancient ruins matching your own technology
- Philosophical undertones for interested players
- Zero impact on gameplay for those who ignore it

### Character-Driven Stories
- **Villager relationships** develop over time
- **Random events** create emergent narratives
- **Quest system** offers optional objectives
- **Seasonal celebrations** build community

## Monetization Strategy (Ethical F2P Model)

### Philosophy
**"Free to fully enjoy, pay for more content and personal expression"**

The base game must be completely playable and enjoyable without spending money. All monetization is:
- **Non-predatory:** No loot boxes, no energy systems, no pay-to-win
- **Transparent:** Clear pricing, no hidden costs
- **Value-focused:** Players get substantial content for money spent
- **Optional:** Never required to enjoy the core experience

### Cosmetic Microtransactions (In-Game Shop)

**Philosophy:** Personal expression only, zero gameplay advantage

#### Villager Customization ($0.99-2.99)
- **Outfit Sets** - Themed clothing for villagers
- **Color Palettes** - New color schemes for buildings
- **Accessories** - Hats, tools, decorative items for villagers
- **Emotes** - Fun animations for villagers

#### Decorative Items ($0.99-4.99)
- **Statue Packs** - Decorative monuments
- **Garden Collections** - Plants, flowers, fountains
- **Lighting Sets** - Lanterns, streetlights, mood lighting
- **Furniture Packs** - Interior decoration items

#### Quality of Life ($1.99-4.99)
- **Extra Village Slots** - Save more village layouts
- **Blueprint Storage** - Unlimited saved blueprints (vs. 10 free)
- **Color Wheel Access** - Full RGB customization (vs. preset palettes)

#### Premium Currency: "Stardust"
- **No predatory pricing** - Can buy exactly what you want
- **Earn through gameplay** - Daily login rewards, achievements
- **Transparent rates:**
  - $4.99 = 500 Stardust
  - $9.99 = 1100 Stardust (10% bonus)
  - $19.99 = 2400 Stardust (20% bonus)
- **Direct purchase option** - Can also buy items with real money directly

#### Battle Pass (Optional Seasonal Track) ($9.99/season)
- **Free Track:** Available to all players with decent rewards
- **Premium Track:** Enhanced rewards, exclusive cosmetics
- **No FOMO mechanics:** Seasonal items return to shop eventually
- **No XP boosts:** Purely cosmetic rewards
- **Duration:** 3 months per season
- **Includes:**
  - 30+ tiers of rewards
  - Exclusive building skins
  - Villager outfits
  - Enough Stardust to buy next pass (if completed)

## Art & Audio Direction

### Visual Style
**Primary Option: Hand-Drawn Doodle Aesthetic**
- **Inspiration:** Doodle God, Kingdom: Two Crowns
- **Characteristics:**
  - Sketch-like line art
  - Watercolor-style fills
  - Charming imperfections
  - Warm, inviting color palette
  - Paper texture backgrounds

**Alternative: Pixel Art**
- **Inspiration:** Stardew Valley, Necesse
- **Characteristics:**
  - 32x32 or 16x16 tiles
  - Limited color palette
  - Smooth animations
  - Nostalgic appeal

### Color Philosophy
- **Khoswa-Kian Orange & Blue:** Signature colors appear in UI
- **Biome-specific palettes:**
  - Meadows: Greens, yellows, soft blues
  - Forest: Deep greens, browns, dappled light
  - Desert: Warm oranges, tans, sunset purples
  - Mountains: Grays, whites, cool blues
  - Ocean: Teals, blues, seafoam greens
- **Warm background option:** Kindle-style warmth slider
- **Paper texture:** Subtle noise for cozy feel

### Audio Design
- **Music Style:** Lo-fi, ambient, pastoral
- **Adaptive Soundtrack:** Changes with biome and time
- **Sound Effects:**
  - Satisfying placement sounds
  - Gentle ambient noises (birds, wind, water)
  - Soft villager chatter
  - No harsh or jarring sounds
- **Audio Options:**
  - Individual volume sliders
  - Ambient-only mode
  - Podcast/music-friendly (minimal audio)

### Core Principles
1. **Clarity over complexity**
2. **Minimal clicks to common actions**
3. **Visual feedback on all interactions**

### Key UI Elements
- **Radial menu** for quick building access
- **Floating resource counter** (non-intrusive)
- **Minimap** with biome indicators

### Menu Design
- **Main Menu:**
  - Scrolling biome background (Necesse-inspired)
  - Social media links
  - Feedback button
  - Patch notes ticker
  - Community showcase rotation
- **In-Game HUD:**
  - Minimal by default
  - Toggle for full information
  - Camera pan toggle
  - Screenshot mode (hide all UI)

## Risk Mitigation

### Market Risks
- **Oversaturated cozy game market**
  - Mitigation: Unique Khoswa-Kian IP, free-to-play model
- **Free-to-play stigma**
  - Mitigation: Transparent, ethical monetization; generous base game

### Development Risks
- **Scope creep**
  - Mitigation: Strict feature prioritization, MVP mentality
- **Technical challenges**
  - Mitigation: Godot community support, proven engine
- **Solo/small team burnout**
  - Mitigation: Realistic timeline, community support, potential contractors

### Business Risks
- **Low conversion rates**
  - Mitigation: Diversified revenue (DLC + cosmetics + battle pass)
- **High server costs**
  - Mitigation: Peer-to-peer multiplayer, optional dedicated servers
- **Negative reception**
  - Mitigation: Beta testing, community feedback loops, iterative development
